<p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Lake Titicaca, the world's highest navigable lake, is arguably the second most visited attraction in Peru, after Machu Picchu. Puno the Folkloric Capital of Peru serves as the gateway to Lake Titicaca. </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Travelers traveling from Cusco to Puno are often muddled in choosing the best and safe mode of transport. In this post, we have compiled the best ways of transport from Cusco to Puno and the pros and cons of choosing them. </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><img class="alignnone size-full wp-image-8100" src="/images/blog/how-to-get-from-cusco-to-puno/B67DZ5BNXNF5RNAM26YJ4NCCWI.jpg-copy.png" alt="Cusco to Puno Bus" width="800" height="533" /></p><h2 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cusco to Puno Bus</strong></h2><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">The cheapest, safest, and most preferred mode of transport from Cusco to Puno is by bus. Many bus companies serve this route, and some have multiple daily departures. The route is also served by busses operating from Cusco to Copacabana or La Paz in Bolivia. </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Yes, this is the best option to reach Puno from Cusco. </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Busses in Peru are relatively safer and due to overgrowing competition among most companies, they go above and beyond in offering optimum service to their passengers. Most of them have reclining seats and some even offer meal services onboard. </span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Busses from Cusco depart from the main Bus Station which is 11 kilometers away from the main square of Cusco, with the exception of Cruz del Sur which departs from its own terminal. The bus terminal in Puno is just 1.7 kilometers away from the main square where most hotels and hostels are located.</span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Pros of traveling by bus from Cusco to Puno:</b></p><ul style="text-align: center;"><li style="text-align: left;" aria-level="1">It is cheaper, safer, and more comfortable</li><li style="text-align: left;" aria-level="1">Choosing a night bus with fully reclining seats would let you get some sleep and you won’t lose time traveling and will be arriving fresh to start your Lake Titicaca Tour.</li><li style="text-align: left;" aria-level="1">Bus terminals in both Cusco and Puno are closer to the city center.</li></ul><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Travel Time:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> 7.5 Hours</span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><b>Price:</b><span style="font-weight: 400;"> 10 - 15 USD (each way)</span></p><p style="text-align: center;"><img class="aligncenter wp-image-8099 size-full" src="/images/blog/how-to-get-from-cusco-to-puno/jc-gellidon-j_5sxxspFtc-unsplash.jpg" alt="Cusco to Puno " width="800" height="534" /></p><h2 style="text-align: center;"><strong>Cusco to Puno Flight:</strong></h2><p style="text-align: center;"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Juliaca’s Inca Manco Capac International Airport (JUL) serves the city of Puno.
