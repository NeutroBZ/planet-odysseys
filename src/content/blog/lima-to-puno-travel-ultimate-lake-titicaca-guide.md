Arrival in Puno: First Impressions and Practical Tips As you arrive in Puno, the altitude may be your first encounter. At 3,827 meters above sea level, it’s significantly higher than Lima. Take it slow on your first day to acclimate. Puno greets you with its vibrant culture and bustling atmosphere. The main square, Plaza de Armas, pulses with life and is a great starting point. Its proximity to Lake Titicaca ensures breathtaking experiences are at your doorstep. Start your exploration with the Uros Floating Islands. These man-made islands offer a glimpse into ancient traditions, with homes built from totora reeds. This unique lifestyle has continued for centuries. Next, head to Taquile Island, where vibrant textiles and a distinct Quechua culture are on display. The island's craftspeople are renowned for their meticulous woven goods. The Plaza de Armas is Puno's central hub. Surrounded by colonial architecture, it’s a lively meeting point and perfect for people-watching. For those interested in history, the Sillustani archaeological site awaits. Its chullpas, or funerary towers, provide insights into pre-Incan societies. Exploring Lake Titicaca: Uros, Taquile, and Amantani Islands Lake Titicaca is a voyage into the heart of Andean culture. With no hotels, visitors stay with host families, offering a deeper cultural connection. Here, explore serene landscapes and vibrant community life. Visiting these islands offers a chance to learn about ancient traditions still alive today. Each island holds its unique stories and customs. Highlights of Lake Titicaca: Uros: Unique floating islands with a rich history. Taquile: Artistic traditions and panoramic views. Amantani: Authentic cultural experiences with local families.
